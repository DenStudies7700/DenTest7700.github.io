2. Bootable USB



- Once you have Rufus downloaded, plug in your USB inside of a USB port in you Laptop/PC.
- Run the rufus*.exe file and then on top, make sure the correct USB is selected.
- Now you have to select your ISO file. In the Boot selection area, hit the button select and select your downloaded ISO file.
- Review everything and once everything is looking good, you can hit Start and hit Ok for every prompting window if shown.

- Once the whole process is done, you should be able to see ready & you are ready to move to the next part.

3. Preparing for Installation



Restart your computer. Keep pressing the button on your keyboard that shows the BIOS screen for your Laptop/PC while the restart accurs until the BIOS screen shows up.
The key is independent on your Laptop or Motherboard manufacture.
Do some research and find the BIOS button. It's usually F12, F11, F1 or In most cases, the button DEL on your keyboard.

Once you are booted inside of your BIOS, there should be a tab named BOOT on top.
The image below shows an example of my own BIOS screen with the BIOS option on top:

- You should see a few options in the boots tab. All you have to do is just put your USB Drive on top of the other boot options so the Motherboard reads the USB/Drive first and it won't boot through your already exist Windows partition.
